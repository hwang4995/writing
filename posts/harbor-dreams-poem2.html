Stellar Dreams

December 8, 2025 · Poetry

After "Harbor Dreams" by Bei Dao

When starlight speckles the sky the night air swallows me cold step by step the crickety ladder pulls me to the sky pulls me to my dreams
I returned to the dock by my home bringing gospel and glasses for the wind the gospel expanded the universe the glasses expanded my universe crickets’ musical legs sing in the revelation of heaven a mollusk slithers leaving parts of its body in the unknown when the waves wash over me my limbs sink into depths of black tea starlight serenely filled my lungs turning me to white dwarf I walked to where sky embraces sea and stopped only to plant a kiss on its cheek
The wind blew upon the sun and moon sailors searched for life on Mars through desperate telescope true, I’m not an astronaut but created to be celestial so I’ll become dust on the side of a spaceship like a snail to a leaf and take off to return home
