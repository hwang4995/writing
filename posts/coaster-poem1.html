The Blue Coaster

September 28, 2025 · Poetry

My mother pressed a blue coaster– which she crocheted herself– into my hands, as she placed my freshly-made green tea on the table.
A small square rug holes lined along knots, thin-haired caterpillars loosely intertwined, braids absorbing sky blue.
I brought the soft graph paper as I moved to Ithaca, fibrous screams of history, documenting a mother’s love.
And I look upon the blue chromis hooked on the wall, a moving canvas of memories, of mung bean soup, of home, of my mother.
